Strategic support every church needs.

Four core competencies I bring to churches and nonprofits. Each matters on its own; together they form a coherent strategy.

AI strategy & responsible adoption

Most teams are evaluating dozens of AI tools without a clear sense of which to trust. I help leaders cut through the noise, identifying where generative AI and automation create real value, where they don't, and how to roll out the right pieces in a way that protects your data, your voice, and your culture. The 40-60% reduction in manual work is usually a side effect of getting clarity first.

Donor development & retention

Acquisition is expensive; retention is where the real story lives. I work with development teams on donor journey design, automated stewardship, and predictive analytics (drawing on behavioral data, not just demographics) to lift retention and surface major-gift prospects already in your database.

Accounting automation & insight

Finance teams at mission-driven organizations are buried in manual reconciliation, slow accounts payable, and revenue tracking that lags real-world activity. I help leverage AI tools to streamline AP, automate reconciliation, accelerate close, and surface revenue insights in real time, so finance becomes a source of clarity for leadership instead of a bottleneck.

Digital ministry & engagement

Churches and nonprofits today serve people across multiple campuses, time zones, and screens. I help teams design digital discipleship platforms, virtual ministry experiences, and engagement systems that meet people where they actually are, without losing the human texture of the work.

Speaking & teaching

I regularly speak with church and nonprofit leaders on the practical realities of integrating AI into mission-driven work: what's been useful, what's been overhyped, and the questions worth asking before adopting new technology. Available for conferences, leadership retreats, board sessions, and staff workshops.
